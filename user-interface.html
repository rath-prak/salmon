Tandem

Group project, user interface design and front end development

Tandem is a ridesharing app that allows drivers and passengers willing to travel together to connect and share the costs of the journey. (I have more examples of UI design in the graphic design section)

Introduction

For our final week at Enspiral Dev Academy, each student was required to pitch an idea for final group projects, each team had six days to complete the project.

Our team consisted of 6 members and the chosen idea was Tandem — a ridesharing app that connects drivers and passengers willing to travel together and share the costs. Tandem was initially aimed at drivers traveling long distances looking to fill empty seats to reduce travel costs, but we later included adventurous travelers looking to save money.

As a team, we were all involved with product development and planning. My additional role was to design and implement the user interface of the app as well as front end development.

Technology

Tandem used many different technologies. We used Express to run a server and Knex to assist with our SQL calls to our PostgreSQL database. We used handlebars for html templating, SASS for css, Jquery for DOM manipulation and nightwatch, selenium and OAuth for unit testing and authorisation. We deployed the final app to Heroku.

Our approach and process

We started with planning, developed user stories and figured out what technologies were best. We then moved to building wireframes for the site. Creating wireframes, user stories, and sitemaps from the start were important because it ensured that every team member was on the same page which made delegating roles and tasks straightforward.

We used InDesign to produce the wireframes and site maps which were bases for my UI design. Since we had less than a week to complete the app, we skipped the prototype stage and decided to move straight into development.

(Wireframes done with indesign)

Welcome to Tandem

At midnight on the final day, there was a 'code freeze', which meant no more coding or improvements to the app. After 6 days of intense development and design, we had achieved our MVP and felt we had an app that we could proudly present.

There were still many areas that still needed work such as adding additional features and refactoring code. I would have liked to have done more iterations and improvements on the UI design, the app was not yet fully responsive and the CSS needed cleaning up. Everyone in the team had an amazing experience.

Te Awe Maori Business Network

Te Awe Wellington Māori Business Network is a non-profit organisation and was established in 1996. It is the oldest Māori Business Network operating in New Zealand.

Te Awe wanted to update their existing website so it would appeal more existing and new members. I was responsible for the redesign and front-end development of the site. For the UI/UX I focused on a modular approach which would allow the client to easily expand and update the site by reusing existing modules. I also used Zurb Foundation for my CSS framework to ensure the final website was completely responsive across all platforms. Click here to visit Te Awe.
